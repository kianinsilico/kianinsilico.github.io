Navigating the Internship Search

Erasmus+ Traineeships and Independent Research

Finding a laboratory and a supervisor for your thesis or research stay can feel overwhelming. This guide is designed to help bioinformatics students structure their search, prepare their applications, and identify potential destinations.

1. Funding Options

There are a three different pathways to fund your stay abroad:

i.   You can apply for funding under the standard Erasmus+ traineeship call.

ii.  Alternatively, you can apply for the Tesi all'Estero Fabit which is FaBiT’s study grants specifically designed for Thesis Preparation Abroad.

iii. Sometimes, you can find paid internship positions, although these are naturally quite rare.

2. The Time Frame

Timing is critical when applying for research positions.

  • If you plan to begin your Erasmus+ Traineeship after the third semester, mid-March to April is the right time to start.
  • For research stays that are not part of any mobility program, you still have some time.
  • I suggest starting your search now to become familiar with the process. However, September onward is definitely the optimal time to actively apply.

If you are going for the FaBiT study grants for the preparation of the thesis abroad (Tesi all'Estero Fabit), depending on your start date of your internship, the timeframe varies:
    - June/July: If you are planning to start in January or later;
    - December: If your are planning to start in May or later.

3. Application Materials

Before you start emailing professors, ensure your documents are prepared professionally.

  • You will need an updated CV and a well-written cover letter.
  • Try to personalize your cover letters specifically for the laboratories that are your top priorities.
  • Mention their recent projects, published papers, or ongoing research.
  • Explain how the specific methods they use are relevant to your own academic background and experience.
  • This targeted approach demonstrates that you are genuinely interested and not just blindly sending automated applications.

4. Potential Destinations

If you are unsure where to start looking, here are a few solid strategies and institutions to consider:

  • You can review the winter school webpage at https://www.biocomp.unibo.it/~school20XX/, replacing the “XX” with the specific year you want to check.
  • There, you will find lists of professors who could potentially become your supervisor.
  • These professors are a great starting point because they are already familiar with the University of Bologna and our master’s program.
  • Keep in mind that some countries, such as France, are required by law to pay their interns.
  • Because of this, you might be able to secure paid positions at institutions like the University of Côte d’Azur.
  • The Pasteur Institute also publicly lists potential projects and laboratories seeking interns under the Erasmus program.
  • They offer projects related to Bioinformatics and AI, though these mainly focus on infectious diseases.
  • The Centre for Genomic Regulation in Barcelona occasionally offers paid positions and has previously hosted bioinformatics students from Unibo.
  • Finally, you could look into the Rostlab at the Technische Universität München.
